## 1 页 research concept note 结构

### Background

Age-friendly built environments are increasingly important due to population ageing. Existing environmental evaluation methods rely heavily on manual expert assessment and lack scalable, multimodal and predictive spatial intelligence.

### Gap

Current indoor scene understanding datasets and models focus on geometry, objects and navigation, but rarely encode age-friendly design requirements, fall risk, caregiving space, accessibility and human-centered spatial interventions.

### Aim

To develop a domain-specific multimodal spatial world model that can understand, evaluate, predict and optimize age-friendly indoor environments.

### Objectives

1. Construct an age-friendly indoor spatial dataset with visual, geometric, semantic and expert-evaluation labels.
2. Develop a multimodal model for indoor scene understanding and age-friendly risk assessment.
3. Build a scene-graph-based representation of accessibility, furniture relations and caregiving affordances.
4. Generate design interventions for safer and more accessible indoor layouts.
5. Validate the model using expert evaluation and real residential/eldercare cases.

### Methods

- Computer vision
- 3D scene understanding
- Vision-language models
- Scene graph
- Rule-based + AI hybrid risk evaluation
- Generative layout optimization
- Expert validation

### Expected Contribution

- A domain-specific age-friendly indoor spatial dataset.
- A multimodal spatial understanding model for interior environments.
- A risk and accessibility evaluation framework.
- A generative design recommendation prototype.
- A bridge between interior design research and computer science spatial intelligence.
